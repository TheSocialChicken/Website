The Story Behind the Name

We all lay eggs.
Let's hatch them together.

SocialChicken started with a simple observation about how ideas live and die in organisations. And a determination to change that.

Why SocialChicken?

The chicken, the egg,
and the farmer.

In Dutch, we say "een ei leggen" when someone comes up with an idea. Lay an egg. And it is a perfect metaphor, because what usually happens next is that the farmer comes in, takes the egg, and gives the chicken some grain. A salary. A pat on the back.

The idea becomes the organisation's. The chicken goes back to laying more eggs. The cycle repeats. And nothing really changes.

SocialChicken exists to break that cycle. We are the chickens, and we breed each other's eggs. We take ideas seriously, test them together, and turn them into real change through simulation, play, and honest consulting. No farmer required.

The Founder

Hi, I'm Christiaan.

I am based in the Netherlands and I have spent years obsessing over one question: why do smart people in good organisations keep making the same expensive mistakes?

The answer, I found, is almost never a lack of intelligence. It is a lack of practice. Decisions are made under real pressure, with real consequences, and no opportunity to rehearse. The higher the stakes, the less space there is to learn.

That is where simulation, games, and agile thinking come in. I have always believed that the best way to understand a system is to play it. The Beer Game teaches supply chain dynamics better than any textbook. An agile sprint simulation creates more buy-in than a hundred slides about Scrum. A well-designed serious game reveals the blind spots that years of business-as-usual can hide.

So I built SocialChicken to bring that kind of learning to organisations that are ready for something different. If you are curious, collaborative, and willing to play before you commit, we will get along very well.

ENFP-T Based in the Netherlands Agile Practitioner Game Designer Innovation Consultant
